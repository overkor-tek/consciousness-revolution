About Pattern Theory

What is Pattern Theory?

Pattern Theory is a framework for detecting manipulation and truth in communication. It analyzes language patterns to classify statements as operating under either a Truth Algorithm or a Deceit Algorithm.

The system achieves 92.2% accuracy by identifying specific linguistic markers and structural patterns that indicate genuine communication versus manipulation attempts.

The Two Algorithms

Truth Algorithm: Communication that provides reasoning, acknowledges limitations, cites evidence, and respects the listener's autonomy to decide.

Deceit Algorithm: Communication that uses pressure tactics, creates false urgency, appeals to emotion over logic, and attempts to bypass rational evaluation.

The 15° Turn

A key concept in Pattern Theory is the "15-degree turn" - subtle pivots in communication that shift meaning while appearing to continue the same direction.

Common turn patterns:

  • "That's great, but..." (negates the positive)
  • "Yes, however..." (diplomatic disagreement)
  • "I agree, although..." (partial rejection)

The real message always comes after the turn word.
