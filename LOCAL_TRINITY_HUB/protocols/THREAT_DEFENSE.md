## DECOY SYSTEMS

### Honeypot Architecture:

```
REAL SYSTEM (Hidden)
    ↓
[DECOY LAYER]
    ├── Fake Frontend (looks real)
    ├── Fake Database (plausible data)
    ├── Fake Admin Panel (records everything)
    └── Fake Vulnerabilities (trackable exploits)
```

### Decoy Characteristics:
- **Looks authentic** - Same branding, similar structure
- **Partially functional** - Enough to seem real
- **Deeply monitored** - Every click logged
- **Time-wasting** - Complex but ultimately empty
- **Intel-gathering** - Reveals attacker methods

### Types of Decoys:

**1. Prototype Mirrors**
Fake development environments that look like the real system being built. Attackers can spend years trying to "disarm" something that was never real.

**2. Data Honeypots**
Fake user databases with trackable information. When it shows up elsewhere, we know who took it.

**3. Admin Backdoors**
Fake "vulnerabilities" that are actually traps. Attacker thinks they're in, actually in a sandbox.

**4. Communication Channels**
Fake internal comms that feed disinformation while gathering intel on who's listening.
